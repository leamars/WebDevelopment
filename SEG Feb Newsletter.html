Growing SEG

So far, 2013 is proving to be another great year of expansion and consolidation for SEG. Our member base is growing and the Financial Skills program that we began last year is underway. We’ve strengthened the integration of SEG’s opportunities, and we are recruiting volunteers and partners from students and non-students alike. Now, we can really see our program areas becoming mutually reinforcing, where they all make up the holistic operations of SEG that we want to offer to fulfill our mission for the community.
New Space and New Classes

Thanks to a partnership with the Grinnell Chamber of Commerce, SEG can be found at our office space in the Sullivan Bank Building on 4th Avenue in Downtown Grinnell. We also have a new address PO 481, 932 Broad Street, Grinnell, Iowa 50112-9998.
 
The next great addition to the SEG model is our Financial Skills Training, begun with a generous sponsorship from the Sarah Boyer ‘08 Fellowship in the summer of 2012. The program offers our clients personalized information on a variety of financial topics, including budgeting and saving, debt management, consumer protection and credit. Our goal is to empower individuals and families with tools to help them sustainably manage their finances and improve their livelihoods.
 
Since launching the program this fall, we have six SEG consultants and have successfully served three clients. SEG uses an independent consulting model, a flexible approach that offers the best results for our clients. This semester we hope to both expand our client base and integrate community volunteers into our education program.
Credit Reporting

As of February 14th, SEG is officially up and running with credit reporting capability. What a great Valentine’s Day gift! Operating through the Credit Builder’s Alliance, we now have contracts with Experian and Transunion to report the lending activity of loan recipients. With this capacity in place, SEG will start offering credit-building loans which, especially in conjunction with other forms of credit, have the potential to significantly improve our clients’ credit scores. This will allow us to expand how we serve clients and make their financial success even more of a reality.
Fundraising

Thank you to members of the Fundraising and Community Outreach Committees, whose activities have ushered SEG into its most financially successful year to date. A special thanks to our major donors, including the Otto Family Foundation, Grinnell United Way and Lincoln Savings Bank. Thanks also to Professor Monty Roper, whose impressive marathon run raised approximately $7000, and to people from the Grinnell College community and beyond whose generous pledges contributed to making that fundraiser a huge success.
Working with OxFam America

The first week of March, SEG was excited to collaborate with the Grinnell Chapter of OxFam America to present the founders of Cafetin Ninas Moses as a part of the International Women’s Day. Founded by Neryeda Urbina in 2011, the Cafetin (a.k.a. “small cafe”) offers refreshments and event-hosting space for weddings and other community events. We surveyed the four women entrepreneurs’ ideas on the role of women in microfinance and will present their perspectives and our work with them in an open forum.
